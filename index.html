ABOUT

My name is Jude Rea Meraz and I was born in East Los Angeles on July 30, 1973. In my early childhood years I lived in Indio, California. When my father passed away, our family moved around alot, eventually, my mother moved the family to Ontario, California. Having a difficult time of adjusting to moving around so much I became involved in substance abuse and criminal gang activity. I entered a cycle of incarceration, despite this, I never lost my interest in education.
In Pelican Bay's Security Housing Unit, while reading Popular Science Magazine I discovered my interest in Computer Coding. A few years later, arriving in Ironwood State Prison I would get the chance to expand on my education. Having earned Associate's degrees in Behavioral and Social Sciences, Business and Technology, Arts and Humanities, and Math and Science. I dove head first into the The Last Mile (TLM). Becoming proficient in web designing, both front-end and back-end functional computer programming.
Today, I enjoy working with The Last Mile company in their efforts to reach our mission goal of giving back to those who share a passion for computer coding and improving our communities. In the future I plan to work with organizations such as Alternatives to Violence (AVP), Anti-Recividism Coalition (ARC), and Getting Out by Going In (GOGI) to help introduce and sponsor computer literacy related initiatives that will help pass on the knowledge and fascination of computer coding onto our youth.
